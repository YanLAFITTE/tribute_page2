Mestre Bimba

The father of the Capoeira Regional.

Here's a time line of Mestre Bimba's life:

  • 1899 - Mestre Bimba (Manuel dos Reis Machado) was born in Salvador, Brasil.
  • 1928 - Mestre Bimba created the Capoeira Regional.
  • 1932 - He created the "Centro de Cultura Fisica e Luta Regional" (Center for Physical Culture and Regional Wrestling) which then took the name of Capoeira Regional.
  • 1936 - Bimba challenged fighters of several martial arts in order to test his capoeira. He fought against Henrique Bahia, José Custódio dos Santos (Zé I), Américo Ciência and many other people he met in Brazilian ports. Bimba won all his fights.
  • 1937 - The Academy of Mestre Bimba was the first to receive official authorization to teach Capoeira, following a public demonstration before the then president Getúlio Vargas, who revoked the Sampaio Ferraz law, thus legally authorizing capoeira. Bimba wins the educator diploma.
  • 1942 - He opened a second school in the "Terceiro de Jesus" street of "Laranjeiras" (today "Francisco Muniz Barreto" street). The school still exists today and is supervised by one of its students, "Vermelho". He also imported Capoeira into the army and the police.
  • 1949 - Mestre Damião organized Bimba's first trip to São Paulo, for Capoeira demonstrations.
  • 1950 - Bimba travels to Rio de Janeiro and other states.
  • 1954 - He appears in "Vadiação" a film by Alexandre Robatto.
  • 1968 - He appears in "Dança de guerra" the film of his disciple Jair Moura.
  • 1973 - Dissatisfied with the promises and the lack of support from the local authorities in Bahia, he left for Goiânia with his family at the invitation of a former disciple, Oswaldo de Souza.
  • 1974 - Mestre Bimba dies at the age of 75.
  • 1979 - The mortuary remains of Bimba were transferred to Bahia, at the initiative of his disciples, and since 1994 have rested in the ossuary of Ordem Terceira do Carmo.
  • 1979 - The Federal University of Bahia awarded the honorary title of doctor honoris causa to Manoel dos Reis Machado, illiterate black, who died twenty-two years earlier.
